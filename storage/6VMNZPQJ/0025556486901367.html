Permanence in Lotka-Volterra equations: linked prey-predator systems

Author links open overlay panelGabrielaKirlinger
https://doi.org/10.1016/0025-5564(86)90136-7Get rights and content

Abstract

A population-dynamical system is called permanent if all species survive, provided they are initially present. More precisely, a system is called permanent if there exists some level k > 0 such that if the number xi(0) of species i at time 0 ispositive for i = 1, 2, â€¦, n, then xi(t) > k for all sufficiently large times t. In this paper conditions for permanence in prey-predator systems linked by interspecific competition of prey are deduced.
