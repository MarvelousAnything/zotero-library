Every Step Counts

The Current Protocols collection includes over 25,000 step-by-step techniques, procedures, and practical overviews that provide researchers with reliable, efficient methods to ensure reproducible results and pave the way for critical scientific discovery. With its emphasis on carefully curated, highly edited methods rich in detail, practical advice, and troubleshooting, Current Protocols enables researchers to advance their research with an efficiency of time and resources.

Abstract

Cover: RNA isolation is routinely carried out in many laboratories around the world for numerous downstream applications. In this issue, Oñate-Sánchez and Verdonk describe a robust method for RNA isolation from multiple species. The approach, termed the Citrate-Citric Acid RNA-isolation (CiAR) method, is fast and efficient, and requires only two home-made simple, inexpensive, and non-toxic buffers. These buffers, which contain citric acid and its sodium salt, give the method its name.
